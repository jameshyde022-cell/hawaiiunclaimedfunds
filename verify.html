If We Contacted You

If you received a letter, call, email, postcard, or text from Hawaii Unclaimed Funds Recovery, it may be because your name or business appears in publicly available City and County of Honolulu escheated-check records. You do not need to take our word for it. You can verify the record directly on Honolulu.gov by searching the payee name, check amount, or check number. We can also help check the State of Hawaii Unclaimed Property database for additional funds.

How to Verify

Two Ways to Verify — Both Free

You can verify records through two separate government sources. Use both to get the full picture.

1

Verify Honolulu Escheated Checks

Go to the City and County of Honolulu Department of Budget and Fiscal Services escheated-check page. Search by payee name, check amount, or check number. If the record appears there, the check record is public and real. You can decide whether to pursue the claim yourself or request help.

This is the primary source we use when identifying potential payees. The database is publicly available and maintained by the City and County of Honolulu.

Important: This is the City and County of Honolulu database — it is separate from the State of Hawaii unclaimed property database. Do not confuse the two.

2

Check State of Hawaii Unclaimed Property

The State of Hawaii maintains a separate unclaimed-property database. You can search by personal name or business name. This may show additional funds separate from Honolulu escheated checks. You can search and file directly with the State for free.

This database covers a broader range of property types including dormant bank accounts, insurance proceeds, utility deposits, and other abandoned assets. It is maintained separately from the Honolulu escheated-check records.

Note: This is a separate database from the City and County of Honolulu escheated-check records. A person may appear in one, both, or neither.

What May Appear in These Databases

More Types Than Most People Expect

Honolulu escheated checks and State unclaimed property cover a wide range of financial assets.

🏙️

Honolulu Escheated Checks

  • City and County of Honolulu issued checks
  • Uncashed or undelivered checks
  • Checks over $1,000 prioritized
  • Searchable by payee, amount, or check number
🏛️

State Unclaimed Property — Banking

  • Dormant checking & savings accounts
  • Uncashed checks
  • Certificates of deposit (CDs)
  • Safe deposit box contents
💼

State Unclaimed Property — Other

  • Life insurance proceeds
  • Uncashed payroll checks
  • Security deposits (utility, rental)
  • Stocks, dividends, and brokerage accounts
