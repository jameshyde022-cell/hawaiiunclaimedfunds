The Full Process

Step-by-Step: How We Recover Your Funds

1

Free Database Search

Everything begins with a free search of the State of Hawaii unclaimed property database at dollars.ehawaii.gov. You can do this yourself — we encourage it. If you find potential records and want our help, contact us. If you'd like us to do an expanded search for you (including alternative spellings, former addresses, and business names), we can do that at no charge during the consultation.

2

Free Consultation

You contact us by phone, email, or through our contact form. We'll review what was found and explain exactly what's involved in filing the claim for your specific situation. This consultation is completely free and carries zero obligation. Our AI receptionist Leilani is available to answer calls and schedule callbacks around the clock.

No pressure, ever. We'll tell you honestly if your situation is straightforward enough to handle yourself without hiring us.
3

Signed Finder Agreement

If you'd like to proceed, we'll send you a Finder Agreement for your electronic signature. This agreement is governed by Hawaii Revised Statutes §523A-25 and discloses:

  • The exact funds we've identified on your behalf
  • Our 20% contingency fee (the maximum allowed)
  • The full terms of our engagement
  • Your right to cancel within a specified period

You sign digitally from any device — no printing, no office visit, no notary required for this step.

4

Document Collection

We'll provide you with a clear checklist of the exact documents needed for your specific claim type. You upload them securely through our document portal. Common documents include:

  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
  • Social Security card or number verification
  • Proof of address matching the property record
  • For estate claims: death certificate, probate documents
  • For business claims: business license, EIN documentation
5

Notarization (If Required)

Some claims require notarized documents. We facilitate this entirely online through our certified remote online notarization (RON) service — a state-approved method. You connect via video, verify your identity, and sign in front of a licensed notary digitally. This typically takes 15–20 minutes and can be done from your home, office, or anywhere with internet access.

No in-person notary required — online notarization is legally valid in Hawaii.
6

Claim Preparation

Our team prepares a complete, properly formatted claim package to submit to the State of Hawaii. We review all documentation for accuracy and completeness before submission. A thorough, properly assembled claim is the single most important factor in minimizing processing delays.

7

Claim Submission to the State

We submit your complete claim package to the Hawaii Department of Budget and Finance, Unclaimed Property Program. We submit electronically where available, or by certified mail when required. We retain a complete copy of everything submitted for your records and ours.

8

State Review & Follow-Up

The State of Hawaii reviews the claim. Processing times vary by claim type and state workload, but typically range from 6 weeks to 4 months for standard claims, and longer for complex estate or business claims. We monitor the status and proactively follow up with the state if there are any delays or requests for additional information. You don't have to chase anyone — we do that for you.

9

State Approves & Issues Payment to You

When the state approves your claim, they issue payment directly to you — the rightful owner. This comes in the form of a state check or direct deposit (depending on state options available at the time). You will receive a disbursement notice. The State of Hawaii pays you, not us. We never receive or hold your funds at any point.

10

Our Fee — 20% After You're Paid

Only after you receive your disbursement notice from the state does our 20% contingency fee apply. Based on the disbursement notice amount, you pay our fee. If the state denies the claim or you receive nothing, you owe us nothing. This is our unconditional guarantee.

Example: If the State of Hawaii pays you $10,000, our fee is $2,000. You net $8,000 with zero upfront cost and zero paperwork headache.
