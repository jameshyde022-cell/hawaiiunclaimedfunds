The Full Process

Step-by-Step: How We Recover Your Funds

1

Record Review — Two Sources

Our primary lead source is the City and County of Honolulu Department of Budget and Fiscal Services escheated-check database, publicly available at Honolulu.gov. We search by payee name, check amount, and check number, focusing on checks over $1,000.

We also review the State of Hawaii Unclaimed Property database at budget.hawaii.gov for additional funds that may exist separately.

You can do this yourself for free. Both databases are publicly accessible. We encourage you to verify any record directly before contacting us.
2

Free Consultation

You contact us by phone, email, or through our contact form. We'll review what was found, explain where the record appears (Honolulu escheated checks, State database, or both), and explain exactly what's involved in pursuing the claim for your specific situation. This consultation is completely free and carries zero obligation. Our AI receptionist Leilani is available to answer calls and schedule callbacks around the clock.

No pressure, ever. We'll tell you honestly if your situation is straightforward enough to handle yourself without hiring us.
3

Signed Agreement

If you'd like to proceed, we'll send you an agreement for your electronic signature. The agreement discloses:

  • The exact record(s) we've identified and where they appear
  • Our contingency fee terms
  • The full terms of our engagement
  • Your right to cancel within a specified period

Fee agreements may be subject to applicable law. You sign digitally from any device — no printing, no office visit, no notary required for this step.

4

Document Collection

We'll provide you with a clear checklist of the exact documents needed for your specific claim type. You upload them securely through our document portal. Common documents include:

  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
  • Social Security card or number verification
  • Proof of address matching the property record
  • For estate claims: death certificate, probate documents
  • For business claims: business license, EIN documentation
5

Notarization (If Required)

Some claims require notarized documents. We facilitate this entirely online through our certified remote online notarization (RON) service — a state-approved method. You connect via video, verify your identity, and sign in front of a licensed notary digitally. This typically takes 15–20 minutes and can be done from your home, office, or anywhere with internet access.

No in-person notary required — online notarization is legally valid in Hawaii.
6

Claim Preparation

Our team prepares a complete, properly formatted claim package to submit to the appropriate government office. For Honolulu escheated checks, we submit to the City and County of Honolulu Department of Budget and Fiscal Services. For State unclaimed property, we submit to the Hawaii Department of Budget and Finance. We review all documentation for accuracy and completeness before submission.

7

Claim Submission

We submit your complete claim package to the appropriate government office. We submit electronically where available, or by certified mail when required. We retain a complete copy of everything submitted for your records and ours.

8

Review & Follow-Up

The government office reviews the claim. Processing times vary by claim type and workload, but typically range from 6 weeks to 4 months for standard claims, and longer for complex estate or business claims. We monitor the status and proactively follow up if there are any delays or requests for additional information. You don't have to chase anyone — we do that for you.

9

Approval & Payment to You

When the claim is approved, payment is issued directly to you — the rightful owner. This comes in the form of a check or direct deposit from the appropriate government office. You receive the funds, not us. We never receive or hold your funds at any point.

10

Our Fee — Only After You're Paid

Only after you receive your funds does our contingency fee apply. If the claim is denied or you receive nothing, you owe us nothing. Fee agreements may be subject to applicable law. This is our unconditional guarantee — our success depends entirely on your success.

Our fee is charged only after successful recovery. You may also pursue claims directly through the appropriate government office at no cost.
